Steve Jobs

The founder of the Apple

A summary of the life of Steve Jobs:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California
  • 1976 - With his friend Steve Wozniak, co-founds Apple to sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer. A year later, the duo sells the Apple II.
  • 1984 - After seeing the commercial potential of the Xerox Alto, Jobs and Wozniak develop the Macintosh, the first computer mass-produced with a GUI.
  • 1985 - Jobs is forced out of Apple and founds NeXT, and helps develop the visual effects industry by funding a small company known as Pixar.
  • 1997 - Steve Jobs returns to Apple, which is on the verge of bankruptcy. He begins his to develop a line of products such as the Apple Store, iTunes, iMac, iPad, iPod and iPhone.
  • 2001 - Replaces the original Mac OS with Mac OS X, based on the platform designed during his time with NeXT.
  • 2003 - Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic neuroendocrine cancer.
  • 2011 - Dies at the age of 56 from cancer.
